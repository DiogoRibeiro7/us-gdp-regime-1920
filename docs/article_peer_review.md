# Article peer-review checklist

## Major issues to check

- Does the draft claim causality from piecewise regression alone?
- Are Maddison historical estimates clearly distinguished from FRED/BEA national
  accounts?
- Does the article explain why growth rates, not GDP levels, are used for
  above/below-mean labels?
- Are robustness checks reported before strong interpretive claims are made?
- Are historical claims sourceable?

## Minor issues to check

- Are all figures captioned with source and method?
- Are table values rounded consistently?
- Are breakpoints described as statistical evidence rather than historical proof?
- Is the language sober and empirical rather than ideological?
- Are generated article assets referenced by path?

## Required revisions if problems appear

- Replace deterministic language such as `caused by` with careful wording such
  as `coincides with`, `is consistent with`, or `suggests`.
- Add a limitation paragraph if source differences or unstable breakpoints are
  material.
- Add historical citations for every non-obvious contextual claim.

## Optional improvements

- Include a compact robustness visual of recurring break years.
- Include the largest Maddison/FRED growth-difference years in an appendix.
- Add a short data-and-code availability note.

## Recommendation rubric

- Accept if the article keeps claims tied to model output and source evidence.
- Revise if the analysis is technically correct but overstates historical
  interpretation.
- Reject if it treats statistical breakpoints as automatic policy causality.
